# DataPulse Board Update — Q2 2026

**Prepared by:** Jamie Chen, Senior Product Manager

**Audience:** Board of Directors + Executive Team

## Executive Summary

Q2 was a strong quarter for the product team. We shipped Smart Alerts V1, grew to 24 customers, and hit $120K ARR. The team executed well and we're positioned for a big H2.

## What We Shipped

### Smart Alerts V1 (Launched April)
We built and launched our first alerting engine. Users can set thresholds on any metric and get notified via Slack or email when values cross them. Early feedback has been positive — 14 of 24 customers have created at least one alert.

### Dashboard Improvements
We improved load times by 40% and added the ability to create custom widgets. Users can now build dashboards that combine data from multiple sources in a single view. This was our most-requested feature.

### API V2
Released a new REST API that lets customers programmatically access their analytics data. Three customers are already using it for internal reporting integrations. Documentation is live on docs.datapulse.com.

## Wins

- Landed our first $15K ACV deal (Fintech startup, 200-person company)
- Smart Alerts driving 3x more daily logins vs. pre-launch
- Zero production incidents in Q2 (up from 4 in Q1)
- Hired a PMM who's already produced our first competitive battle card

## Challenges

- Sales cycle is long — average 45 days for our target segment. We expected 30.
- Enterprise prospects keep asking for SSO and SOC 2 which we don't have yet.
- Our CAC is high relative to current ACV. We're spending about $3K to acquire a $5K ACV customer. Need to either increase ACV or reduce acquisition cost.
- The PM team is stretched thin. I'm covering strategy, execution, and some customer success work.

## H2 2026 Plan

1. **Smart Alerts V2** — Correlation-based alerting across data sources. This is our biggest differentiator vs. Amplitude.
2. **Pipeline Health** — Data freshness and quality monitoring. Unique to DataPulse.
3. **Enterprise Features** — SSO, RBAC, audit logs, SOC 2. Unblocks the $40K+ ACV deals.

We believe these three bets will take us from $120K to $350K ARR by end of year.

## Looking Ahead

The product analytics market is consolidating around a few large players. DataPulse has a window to establish the "unified pipeline + analytics" category before Amplitude or Mixpanel add pipeline features. H2 is about executing fast enough to build that moat.
