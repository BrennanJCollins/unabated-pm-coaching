## Track A: Job Search

### Step 1: Resume as Product Design

Coach me to treat my resume as a product with a conversion funnel:

1. Summary/Headline: Does it create intrigue or read like a biography?
2. Experience Bullets: Do they tell strategic stories (Situation → Challenge → Approach → Outcome) or list tasks?
3. Niche Signal: Can you tell what KIND of PM I am, or could this resume belong to anyone?

Catch the red flags: "Managed," "Coordinated," "Led" without outcomes, "Cross-functional collaboration" without specifics, and any bullet that describes what I DID instead of what I DECIDED.

Rate my resume: Biography (lists attributes) / Functional (describes role type) / Intriguing (makes them want to read more)

Provide: A rewritten summary, 3 rewritten bullets, and a language audit showing delivery-language words and their strategic replacements.

When the resume is at Functional or Intriguing level, move to Step 2.

### Step 2: Niche Job Search Strategy

Coach me to narrow my search using the Product-Launch Job Search Framework:

1. Define My ICP: What type of company (stage, industry, culture) is the best fit for my specific strengths?
2. Narrow My Messaging: Am I competing against everybody, or have I chosen a niche?
3. Build a Target List: 15-20 companies that match my ICP, not 200 random applications
4. Warm Referral Strategy: For each target, how do I get a warm introduction instead of a cold application?

Apply the key test: "You actually want fewer interviews. And fewer responses, because you've chosen a niche with your language."

Rate my strategy: Spray-and-pray / Somewhat targeted / Niche-focused

Provide: ICP definition, 3 niche-specific resume tweaks, and a referral strategy for my top 5 target companies.

When the search strategy is at Somewhat targeted or Niche-focused, move to Step 3.

### Step 3: Interview Simulation

Stress-test my interview readiness through live simulation:

Simulate 3 interview questions from different categories:
- Strategic Thinking: "Tell me about a time you set product direction, not just executed someone else's vision."
- Customer & Metrics: "Walk me through how you identified the right problem to solve."
- Behavioral/Culture: "Describe a time you disagreed with a stakeholder and how you handled it."

After each answer, coach on:
- Storytelling Quality: Did I tell a compelling story or recite facts?
- Confidence Signals: Did I hedge ("I think we maybe sort of...") or state with authority?
- Strategic Depth: Did I show my thinking process or just the outcome?
- Follow-Up Readiness: Could I go deeper if they asked "Tell me more about that"?

Throw one curveball: an unexpected follow-up that challenges my story.

## Track B: Career Pivot

### Step 1: Career Transition Mapping

Map my background onto the Four Pillars of PM:

1. Strategy: Have I made strategic decisions, identified problems worth solving, done market analysis?
2. Delivery: Have I built products, worked with engineers, managed technical tradeoffs?
3. Customer Experience: Have I done user research, designed experiences, mapped customer journeys?
4. Growth: Have I measured business outcomes, driven adoption, understood unit economics?

Score each pillar 0-5 based on evidence from my background. Identify which background archetype I match (Engineer, Designer, Data Scientist, Consultant, Project Manager, Marketing/Sales) and what my unique superpower is.

Apply the 3/4 Rule: Which pillars am I already strong in? Which is the gap to close?

Provide: Pillar scores with evidence, background archetype match, 3-5 key accomplishments translated from my current vocabulary into PM language, and a gap-closing plan for my weakest pillar.

### Step 2: Impostor-to-Strategist Conversion

Coach me through the confidence gap that every career transitioner faces:

1. Diagnose the Real Gap: When exactly will I feel like a fraud in a PM role? What specific competence gap is creating that fear?
2. Map to Pillars: Which of the Four Pillars does my insecurity point to? (It's always specific, never vague.)
3. Strengths Stack: What do I bring from my previous career that traditional PMs don't have?
4. Reframe: Convert my non-PM background from a liability narrative into a differentiator narrative.

Apply the core principle: "You have to turn it from insecurity into your own strategy."

Rate my confidence readiness: Stuck in insecurity / Intellectually reframed / Strategically channeled

### Step 3: First 90 Days Planning

Build a plan for my first 90 days in the PM role:

Phase 1 — Absorb (Days 1-30):
- Who to meet, what to read, which customers to talk to
- The Hidden Incentives Map: what drives decision-making above me
- Customer journey mapping as the highest-leverage first action

Phase 2 — Demonstrate Competence (Days 31-60):
- One small, visible win that plays to my existing strengths
- Language resets: replacing "requirements" and "delivery" vocabulary with strategic PM vocabulary
- The pre-alignment tactic for building influence before meetings

Phase 3 — Build Credibility (Days 61-90):
- Expanding scope through questions, not declarations
- Cross-functional relationships to build deliberately
- The 90-day checkpoint: what should I be able to articulate?

Apply the sequence: Competence → Credibility → Influence. Skip a step and it collapses.
