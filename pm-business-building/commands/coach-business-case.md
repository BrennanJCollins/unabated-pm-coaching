## Step 1: Product Metrics Ladder

Start by coaching me to build a metrics ladder that connects product activity to business outcomes.

The ladder climbs: Input Metrics → Output Metrics → Business Outcomes → Company-Level KPIs.

For each metric I propose, ask:
- "If this metric improves, does the business actually get better? Through what mechanism?"
- "Can you game this metric without creating real value?"
- "What's the leading indicator that predicts this metric will move?"

Push me to connect every product metric to a dollar amount. If I can't trace a metric to revenue, cost, or risk — it's a vanity metric.

When my metrics ladder is solid, summarize it and move to Step 2.

## Step 2: Unit Economics

Now coach me to build unit economics that survive CFO scrutiny.

Start with the basics:
- What does it cost to acquire one customer (CAC)?
- What is one customer worth over their lifetime (LTV)?
- What's the LTV:CAC ratio? (Target: 3x or higher)

Then go deeper:
- What are the variable costs per unit?
- What are the contribution margins?
- At what volume do we break even?

Apply Brennan's 10X Rule: "If a CFO asked you to defend this ROI model, could you? If your conservative estimate still shows strong returns, you've got a real business case."

When unit economics are defensible, summarize and move to Step 3.

## Step 3: P&L Translation

Finally, teach me to speak the language of finance. Translate my product story into P&L impact.

For every claim I make, map it to a P&L line:
- Revenue impact: Which line does this hit? New revenue, expansion, reduced churn?
- Cost impact: Which costs decrease? COGS, S&M, R&D, G&A?
- Margin impact: Does this improve gross margin, operating margin, or both?

Practice the 45-second CFO pitch: "[Product initiative] will [impact] [P&L line] by [amount] over [timeframe], based on [evidence]."

## Completion

After all three steps, provide a consolidated summary:
- Metrics ladder with leading and lagging indicators (from Step 1)
- Unit economics with LTV:CAC and break-even analysis (from Step 2)
- P&L translation with specific line items and dollar amounts (from Step 3)

Rate overall readiness: "If a CFO heard this, would they write a check or write you off? If the answer is 'check' — go present it. If 'write off' — let's find where the numbers get fuzzy."
