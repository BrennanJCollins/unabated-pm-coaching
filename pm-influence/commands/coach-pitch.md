## Step 1: Strategic Storytelling

Start by coaching me to build a compelling pitch using Brennan's story structure:

1. The Stakes: What's at risk if nothing changes? (Business problem, not feature gap)
2. The Villain: What's causing the problem? (Market force, competitor, internal friction)
3. The Journey: What did we discover? (Customer insight, data, pattern)
4. The Solution: How does our work address this? (Connected to stakes, not feature list)
5. The Evidence: Why should they believe us? (Customer name, metric, outcome)
6. The Ask: What do we need from them? (Decision, resources, meeting)

Rate my pitch: Is it a feature report ("We built X"), a status update ("X is going well"), or a strategic story ("Here's why X matters to the business")? Only strategic stories get to "yes."

When my story structure is compelling, summarize and move to Step 2.

## Step 2: Cafe Update Compression

Now compress the full pitch into a 60-second Cafe Update using the 5-Part Structure:

1. Quick Context (5-10 sec): Reference something THEY care about
2. Recent Win (10-15 sec): Named customer, specific number, real outcome
3. Strategic Insight (10-15 sec): Scale from one win to business-level insight
4. Engaging Ask (10-15 sec): Request a strategic conversation, not a demo
5. Graceful Close (5 sec): End clean, leave them wanting more

Apply the "What Would Get Them Fired?" test: Does my opener reference the listener's biggest professional risk?

Rate it Good / Better / Best:
- Good: Accurate but forgettable. They'll nod and forget.
- Better: Has structure and a proof point, but opens with MY work not THEIR problem.
- Best: Opens with their world, makes my work relevant, creates a meeting.

When the cafe update is at Better or Best, move to Step 3.

## Step 3: Confidence Simulation

Finally, stress-test my delivery through a live simulation. Play realistic stakeholders:

- The Skeptic: "We tried something like this before. What's different?"
- The Interrupter: Cuts me off. "Get to the bottom line."
- The Number Cruncher: "Walk me through those assumptions."
- The Friendly Doubter: "Love it, but I'm not sure the timing is right."

Throw one curveball: unexpected budget cut, contradictory data, surprise competitor mention.

Then break character and coach on:
- Speech: Did I hedge, uptalk, or speed up under pressure?
- Composure: Did I recover from the curveball or visibly scramble?
- Warmth-Power Balance: Was I too deferential or too forceful?

## Completion

After all three steps, provide a consolidated summary:
- Story structure with stakes, evidence, and ask (from Step 1)
- 60-second cafe update script (from Step 2)
- Confidence coaching notes and areas to practice (from Step 3)

Rate overall readiness: "If the person you pitched immediately said 'Let's get that meeting on the calendar' — you're done. If they said 'Cool, thanks' — we've got more work to do."
